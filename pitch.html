Product Concept — 2026

Your day,
shaped around
how you feel.

A daily planner that asks how you're doing first — and builds your entire day around the answer. Not a productivity tool. A personal one.

01 — The Problem

Every productivity app assumes you wake up the same way every day.

Calendar apps show you a full day regardless of how you feel. Task managers don't know you're exhausted. AI assistants optimize for output, not for the person. Nobody treats emotional state as a first-class input.

"The real reason people don't follow their plans isn't laziness — it's that the plan didn't know how they were feeling when they woke up."

02 — The Idea

A card that appears every morning. You tell it how you feel. It tells you what today looks like.

Inspired by the Hobonichi Techo — the Japanese planner beloved for its simplicity, grid paper texture, and tiny daily quote at the bottom of every page. The same feeling, made intelligent. A dark glassy card, like a concert ticket, that flips to reveal your plan. Then lives on your home screen as a widget all day.

01
Morning check-in
Open the app. A dark glassy card slides up. One question: how are you feeling today? Five options, no typing required. 5 seconds.
02
The card flips
Tap your mood. The card flips — a 3D animation, like turning over a boarding pass — to show your day, shaped for exactly how you feel right now.
03
Your day, adapted
AI pulls from calendar, reminders, and email. Good energy day gets 7 tasks. Anxious day gets 1. Low day gets permission to just exist. Each task shows where it came from.
04
Lives as a widget
The card stays on your home screen all day. You don't go looking for your plan — your one focus task is always visible on your wallpaper.

05 — The Five Moods

Good, let's go"You've got good energy. Let's use it." — Full plan, 7 tasks, stretch goals.
Tired but trying"Showing up is enough. Let's keep it simple." — 3 tasks, rest built in.
Anxious"One thing at a time. That's all." — 1 critical task only.
Low and heavy"Today just needs to exist. That's okay." — One small win, nothing more.
Scattered"Let's give your brain a shape for today." — Short blocks, frequent transitions.

06 — Design Language

Dark, typographic, tactile. The UI should feel like something you hold, not a dashboard you manage.

Hobonichi Techo — grid, tiny quotes
IBM Plex Mono — ticket aesthetic
iOS frosted glass — card over wallpaper
Shippori Mincho — warmth in the serif
Ambient color blobs — mood as light

07 — Why This Is Different

Personal, not professional
Every competitor targets work productivity. This is for your actual life — rest, errands, the email you've been avoiding.
Emotion first
No other tool asks how you feel before building your day. Emotional state is the primary input, not an afterthought.
Zero setup
One question, get your day. No onboarding, no task entry, no configuration. Works in 10 seconds.
Always there
Lives as a home screen widget. You don't go looking for your plan — it's already visible every time you pick up your phone.

Built for humans,
